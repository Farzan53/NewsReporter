Islamabad United’s Naseem Shah foresees two foreign players to ‘light up’ PSL 10

KARACHI: Islamabad United’s frontline pacer Naseem Shah on Thursday, expressed his excitement with his team’s two foreign players, Riley Meredith and Matthew Short, ahead of the upcoming 10th edition of the Pakistan Super League (PSL). The three-time champions, who had the last pick in the first round of the platinum category, roped in top-order Australia batter Short, who boasts a vast experience in the shortest format..

TIslamabad United to celebrate PSL 9's title victory next month

Islamabad United will celebrate their Pakistan Super League (PSL) season victory next month in Islamabad, the country's capital. Shadab Khan-led side defeated Multan Sultans by two wickets in the final at Karachi's National Bank Stadium on March 18 to bag their third title, the most by any team in competition's history The celebration will be held on May 2 where the side will parade the trophy from Shalimar Ground to F-9 Park. A special ceremony will be held at F9 Park. The local players and coaching staff of Islamabad will participate in the celebration of the victory. .

Naseem Shah welcomes Riley Meredith and Matt Short to Islamabad United

Islamabad United's star pacer Naseem Shah has expressed excitement over the addition of Australian cricketers Riley Meredith and Matt Short to the team for the upcoming season. In a press release, Naseem praised Meredith’s raw pace and bounce, predicting he will captivate fans and "light up the league." "Pakistani fans are passionate about fast bowling, and we love express pace—Riley fits that perfectly," said Naseem.
