The Impact Of Female Politicians On Legislative Reforms In Pakistan

The history of women in politics in Pakistan is complex. Various Pakistani women have played important political roles over time, breaking barriers and pushing changes that not only addressed issues specific to women but also influenced the sociopolitical structure of the nation. Their contributions cover a broad spectrum of topics, ranging from international diplomacy and constitutional revisions to women's rights and education. Beyond various obstacles, including institutional, political, and cultural hurdles, their efforts have resulted in notable progress in fields like social welfare, human rights, and gender equality. Important personalities in Pakistan's initial years were Fatima Jinnah and Begum Rana Liaquat Ali Khan, who served the nation with their unbeatable efforts. Fatima Jinnah supported her brother's struggle for the freedom of Pakistan and worked for women's empowerment while Begum Rana Liaquat Ali Khan began the establishment of the All Pakistan Women's Association in 1949 in an effort to develop women's facilities for health care, education, and employment.

Is Pakistan on the Brink of Another Political Showdown?

ISLAMABAD: The Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) rally in Islamabad on Sunday has created a new round of controversy in Pakistan, sparking fears that political tensions in the country are set to explode. At the rally, PTI leaders warned the government that they would free party chief and former Prime Minister Imran Khan if he wasn’t legally released from prison within two weeks. The party has also warned of serious consequences if the cases against Khan are taken to military courts. Khan has been in prison since 2023 after being sentenced in a corruption case. Concerns are growing that he could be tried in military courts for his alleged involvement in inciting violent attacks on military installations on May 9 last year. In this regard, the ongoing military trial involving former spy chief Lt. Gen. Faiz Hameed has added another layer of complexity to the country’s political landscape.
