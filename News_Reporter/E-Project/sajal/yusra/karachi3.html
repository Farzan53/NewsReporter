Menzies Aviation opens first global support offices in Pakistan

Menzies Aviation has inaugurated its first global support offices in Pakistan marking a significant milestone in the company’s commitment to foster growth and innovation in the region. The new offices, located in Islamabad, will serve as a hub for Menzies Aviation’s IT and service operations.

Punjab launches Rs 132 billion business finance, card schemes

Chief Minister Maryam Nawaz Sharif has launched the Punjab Easy Business Finance Scheme and Easy Business Card Scheme, with combined allocations of over Rs 132 billion. Speaking at the launch, she highlighted that these initiatives aim to promote self-employment and support small businesses across the province. Under the Punjab Easy Business Finance Scheme, Rs 84 billion has been allocated to provide over Rs 36 billion in interest-free loans to beneficiaries. Loans of up to Rs 1 million each will be available to men, women, transgender persons, and individuals with special needs aged 25 to 55 residing in Punjab.

Finance minister meets Malaysian PM, Chief Advisor BD on sidelines of WEF.

Federal Minister for Finance and Revenue, Muhammad Aurangzeb on Wednesday met with Prime Minister of Malaysia, Anwar Ibrahim and Bangladesh Interim Government’s Chief Advisor, Dr. Muhammad Yunus on the sidelines of the Annual Meeting of the World Economic Forum in Davos. The minister attended the opening dinner of the World Economic Forum Annual Meeting 2025, hosted by Professor Klaus Schwab, Founder of the World Economic Forum (WEF) and Hilde Schwab, Chairperson of the Schwab Foundation for Social Entrepreneurship, said a press release. The finance minister also met with Professor Klaus Schwab, Hilde Schwab and Anita Zaidi, President of Gender Equality at the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation.

Economic sectors

The Pakistan Business Council was established in 2005 by fourteen of the country’s leading Corporates and Business Groups as an advocacy forum to improve the general business environment of the country.



Pakistan's business sector is made up of agriculture, industry, and services. The country's business culture is hierarchical, with seniority and age often guiding meetings and negotiations. Economic sectors Agriculture: In 2023, agriculture contributed about 23.37% to Pakistan's GDP. Industry: In 2023, industry contributed about 20.76% to Pakistan's GDP. Services: In 2023, services contributed over half of Pakistan's GDP. Manufacturing Pakistan's manufacturing sector is dominated by textiles, food, beverages, tobacco, coke and petroleum, and pharmaceuticals. Exports Pakistan's export economy is largely based on textiles and agriculture. Imports Pakistan's imports include machinery, chemicals, and mineral products, primarily petroleum. Business environment Pakistan is considered business friendly, with favorable business laws and tax incentives. Pakistan's stock market is considered to be Asia's best performing. Business culture In Pakistani business culture, hierarchy is important. Seniority and age often guide meetings and negotiations. Respect the most senior person by using titles like "Sir" or "Madam".
