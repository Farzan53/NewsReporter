Operational Training Held for Selected Young Doctors at Maryam Nawaz Health Clinic

Lahore: An operational training session was organized at a local hotel for young doctors selected for the Maryam Nawaz Health Clinics. During the session, the doctors were thoroughly briefed on the complete operational management procedures of the clinics. Punjab Health Minister met the young doctors and congratulated them on their selection and the assignment of responsibilities at the Maryam Nawaz Health Clinics. Speaking at the training session, Provincial Health Minister Khawaja Imran Nazir stated that under the vision of the Chief Minister of Punjab, efforts are being made to empower doctors financially. He clarified that Basic Health Units (BHUs) have not been privatized but outsourced to provide modern and improved healthcare services to the community. Khawaja Imran Nazir further highlighted that the Maryam Nawaz Health Clinics are state-of-the-art facilities and offer a golden opportunity for doctors to make this model successful. He assured that doctors would be provided additional benefits if the outsourcing model proves successful. The clinics have been handed over to young doctors based on merit, experience, and professional expertise. According to Nadia Saquib, young doctors will be handed over the Maryam Nawaz Health Clinics this week, with regular operations commencing from Monday. The Secretary of Health instructed health CEOs to ensure that the young doctors face no issues in their work. Dr. Khalil, representing the PD IRMNCH program, was also present at the training session

PIA flight lands on wrong runway amid heavy fog

LAHORE: A Pakistan International Airlines (PIA) flight narrowly avoided disaster when the pilot allegedly landed on the wrong runway at Lahore Airport in adverse weather conditions. Flight PK-150, originally scheduled from Dammam to Multan, was diverted to Lahore due to heavy fog and poor visibility in Multan. Upon arrival in Lahore, the aircraft was reportedly landed on runway 36L instead of the designated main runway. According to sources, the runway lights were switched off at the time of the landing, exacerbating the potential risk. The PIA administration has taken swift action, grounding both the captain and the first officer. .

How Lahore’s Smog Crisis Endangers Women’s Health and Fertility

Lahore’s smog is a toxic blend of fine particles like PM2.5 and PM10, harmful gases such as nitrogen oxides, sulfur dioxide, and carbon monoxide, along with black carbon, volatile organic compounds, and construction dust. These pollutants come from vehicles, which have increased by 9 percent in the last five years, industrial emissions, coal-burning brick kilns, and open agricultural burning in Pakistan and across the border in India.



DC strengthens influenza surveillance in Pakistan through a cooperative agreement with the Pakistan National Institute of Health (NIH). Since 2004, five surveillance sites were established to conduct surveillance for influenza-like illness in outpatient settings. These sites also conduct surveillance for severe acute respiratory illness among hospitalized patients. Pakistan is the only country where extensively drug resistant typhoid fever is regularly occurring. CDC works with local government, Field Epidemiology Training Program, and health security partners to expand surveillance for typhoid and waterborne diseases. CDC also enhances typhoid prevention and response efforts, and engages in several projects related to bacterial diseases. Laboratory strengthening The Global Laboratory Leadership Program (GLLP) began its first phase in Pakistan in 2019. GLLP strengthens the country's laboratory leadership and management capabilities, which are key functions in public health preparedness and response. The first cohort of GLLP in Pakistan graduated in 2023. Four of Pakistan’s five disease surveillance sites established on-site laboratory capabilities for influenza testing to enhance rapid detection of influenza outbreaks. Workforce development CDC has supported Pakistan's Field Epidemiology and Laboratory Training Program (FELTP) since 2006. The Pakistan FELTP includes two tiers: FELTP-Advanced prepares epidemiologists for national-level public health leadership. FELTP-Frontline trains health workers as community-level epidemiologists. FELTP has been a key technical asset during several health emergencies and was instrumental in establishing Pakistan’s first hepatitis surveillance sites. Ownership of the FELTP was officially transferred to the Government of Pakistan.
