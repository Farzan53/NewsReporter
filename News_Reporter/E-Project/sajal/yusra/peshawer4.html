Horse & Cattle Show in Peshawar from 8th

According to a communication, Chief Minister Ali Amin Gandapur will inaugurate the event, featuring free entry for the public and a vibrant lineup of traditional sports, cultural performances, and family activities. The organisers said the show, which will be held from November 8 to 10, promises an array of attractions, including horse and pigeon races, kabaddi, wrestling, camel and dog races, and camel dances. Over 1,000 horses are expected to compete, while traditional music, art exhibitions, poetry recitals, vintage car displays, and a family festival will further highlight the region’s rich heritage. The opening ceremony will feature traditional music, air gliding, and dance performances.

Peshawar Zalmi prolong sponsorship alliance with Independent

The Pakistan Super League (PSL) team, Peshawar Zalmi have inked a partnership renewal with a web-based Urdu news service based in Pakistan, Independent Urdu. As a result of this arrangement, the brand will remain the team’s digital news partner for season 9. The team via its social media handles, announced the extension with the caption, “We are proud to continue our partnership with Independent Urdu as our Digital News Partner for #HBLPSL9 ⚡️”

Volleyball, Kabadi among other traditional sports becoming victim of social media onslaught

PESHAWAR Volleyball and Kabadi- once famous sports of Khyber Pakthunkhwa are gradually becoming a victim of internet and social media onslaught. With each passing month, Volleyball and Kabadi among others traditional games are diminishing due to social media addiction in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Ehsan Ullah and Shabir Ahmed, the national volleyball and kabadi players of Khyber Pakthunkhwa, are extremely disheartened by decline in traditional sports activities in his hometown of Amankot, Nowshera District due to onslaught of an internet and social media in Pakistan. .
