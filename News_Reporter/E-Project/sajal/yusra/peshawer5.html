Health advisor reviews PHSA training program

PESHAWAR - Ihtesham Ali, Advisor to the Chief Minister on Health, conducted an unannounced visit to the Provincial Health Services Academy (PHSA) to evaluate the mandatory four-month training program for management cadre doctors seeking promotion from Grade 18 to Grade 19. During the visit, he engaged with trainee doctors and reviewed the progress of their training. Dr. Abdul Waheed, Director General of PHSA, provided an overview of the academy’s operations and the ongoing training initiatives under the Human Capital Investment Program. The advisor also inspected the construction projects at the facility and highlighted the importance of strengthening PHSA to enhance the province’s healthcare workforce capacity.

Health screening of special children begins

JHANG: The health screening of 1,015 special children/students studying in various public educational institutions across the district started on Tuesday. Special health screening teams, supervised by DHQ and THQ hospitals, paediatricians, consultant ophthalmologists, dental surgeons, clinical physiologists, speech therapists, audiology therapists, optometric scientists and other health experts, were completing the task. The health teams will provide necessary medication and other services while visiting different educational institutions for special children across the district. On the first day, the DHQ Hospital team organized a health screening camp at Government Special Education School Satellite Town to screen the special children (boys and girls). Dr Umbreen Sarwar supervised the health screening of the special children.

Respiratory Care Week Celebrated at Khyber Medical University Peshawar

PESHAWAR Khyber Medical University (KMU), Peshawar, celebrated Respiratory Care Week under the theme “Breathe Easy: Journey Together, Impact Forever.” This week event emphasized the significance of respiratory therapy, promoting positive mental and physical health with messages like “Inhale Positivity, Exhale Negativity” and “Venting Beyond Patient Care.” The event’s chief guest, Prof. Dr. Rubina Nazli, Dean of Basic Medical Education and Pro Vice Chancellor of KMU, highlighted the critical role of respiratory therapy in modern healthcare. Dr. Rubina underscored the importance of respiratory therapy in patient care, noting the advancements in respiratory equipment and the impact of these services on patient quality of life. She also emphasized the need to raise awareness about the latest models of respiratory care equipment, aiming to enhance patient care practices across healthcare settings. .

How Pakistan’s Peshawar has emerged as mpox 'epicentre'

Pakistan’s Peshawar city is emerging as the ‘epicentre’ of the globally concerning mpox virus. The neighbouring country reported its fifth monkeypox case on Sunday, after a 47-year-old man tested positive at the Bacha Khan International Airport. The latest mpox case has raised concerns about the virus’s spread from travellers arriving from West Asia
